Rendered People in Context (RPiC) Dataset

Abstract

Deep neural network--based models benefit greatly from fully supervised, large-scale, and high quality datasets. However, there exists no such publicly available large-scale dataset for full-body people compositing and factorization tasks. Existing works for inverse graphics factorization focus on general scenes, human faces, or people with no background. To facilitate full-body human factorization and compositing tasks, we build a large-scale dataset, Rendered People in Context, by rendering real 3D-scanned people into real-world High Dynamic Range (HDR) panoramic images. Our dataset features full-body people in various poses, under diverse lighting conditions, with realistic shadows, and in complex environments. It captures the complexity of real photos of people in the wild. To our knowledge, this is the first inverse graphics dataset with realistic full-body people in context.
